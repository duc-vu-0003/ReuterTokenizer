DANISH CROPS TWO WEEKS BEHIND , OFFICIAL REPORT Danish crops are up to two weeks behind normal growth levels due to the cold weather in May and June , when mean temperatures were up to four centigrade below average , the State Plant Cultivation Bureau said in a report .
At the season 's first crop test on June 26 , the bureau rated crops at an overall 94 , the same as on June 20 last year .
The figure of 100 represents normal in a year of average growth conditions with no crop damage .
The test gave ratings expressed as a factor of 100 , as follows - winter wheat 96 -LRB- last year 91 -RRB- , winter rye 97 -LRB- 98 -RRB- , winter barley 92 -LRB- 90 -RRB- , spring barley 94 -LRB- 98 -RRB- , oats 96 -LRB- 98 -RRB- , fodder and sugar beets 87 -LRB- 96 -RRB- , winter rapeseed 98 -LRB- 94 -RRB- , spring rapeseed 94 -LRB- 98 -RRB- .
Final harvest figures for 1986 were not yet available .
